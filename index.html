Autonomy as Infrastructure

Autonomous robotics is entering a new era. The challenge is no longer whether machines can move. The challenge is whether systems can coordinate, scale, and operate reliably across domains.

Elad Bloch approaches robotics as infrastructure rather than hardware. This means:

  • Designing autonomy layers
  • Enabling orchestration between machines
  • Prioritizing resilience over spectacle
  • Building long-term deployable platforms

Infrastructure thinking separates prototypes from enduring systems.
